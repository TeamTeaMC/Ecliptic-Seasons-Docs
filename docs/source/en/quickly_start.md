# Ecliptic Seasons – Quick Start Guide

Welcome to Ecliptic Seasons.

Ecliptic Seasons introduces a seasonal system based on the Twenty-Four Solar Terms. As time passes, the world gradually changes through seasonal transitions, affecting daylight duration, weather, vegetation colors, farming conditions, and winter landscapes.

This guide covers the most important features to help you get started.

------

# Solar Terms and Seasons

A year in Ecliptic Seasons is divided into twenty-four Solar Terms.

As the seasons progress, you may notice:

- Changes in day and night length
- Gradual warming or cooling of the climate
- Snowfall in certain biomes
- Seasonal vegetation color changes
- Seasonal particles and ambient sounds

Whenever a new Solar Term begins, a notification will appear in chat.

------

# Growth Detector

If you plan to do any farming, one of the first tools you should craft is the:

**Growth Detector**

Right-click a crop to view:

- Current growth chance
- Greenhouse level
- Whether environmental requirements are met

When crops grow slowly, the Growth Detector is usually the fastest way to find out why.

------

# Seasonal Farming

When seasonal farming is enabled, crops may perform differently depending on the season.

This does not necessarily mean crops cannot grow. Instead, seasonal conditions affect their natural growth rate.

By default:

- Seasons affect crop growth chance
- Humidity affects crop growth chance
- Bone Meal remains usable

Even under less-than-ideal conditions, farming is generally still possible.

------

# Greenhouses

If you want a more stable farming environment, consider building a greenhouse.

A greenhouse is essentially an enclosed space recognized by the mod.

Once detected, it can:

- Improve farming conditions
- Reduce seasonal penalties
- Work together with humidity mechanics

For long-term farming, greenhouses are often the most effective solution.

------

# Humidity Control

Some crops are sensitive to humidity.

You can adjust greenhouse humidity using the following devices:

## Humidity Tank

Increases humidity within the surrounding area.

Useful for dry environments and crops that prefer higher humidity.

## Greenhouse Dehumidifier

Reduces humidity within the surrounding area.

Useful in overly humid environments.

If you are unsure about local humidity conditions, use a Humidity Meter to check them.

------

# Season Cores

Season Cores are important components of the greenhouse system.

If you want to grow crops outside their preferred season, you will usually need the appropriate Season Core to modify seasonal conditions inside the greenhouse.

------

# How Do I Obtain a Season Core?

Season Cores can be obtained through several methods:

- Advancement rewards
- Completing a Seasonal Prayer Ritual
- Occasionally purchasing one from a Wandering Trader

They are not required for normal farming, but are usually essential for out-of-season cultivation.

------

# Winter and Snow Cover

As winter arrives, the world gradually develops snowy scenery.

By default:

- Snow cover is primarily a visual effect
- No blocks are modified
- Large numbers of real snow layers are not generated

This approach offers better performance and compatibility.

If you prefer traditional snow accumulation, you can enable vanilla-style snow generation in the configuration.

------

# Heatstroke

In hot biomes, prolonged exposure to the midday summer sun may cause **Heatstroke**.

You can avoid it by:

- Staying in shaded areas
- Avoiding long periods of direct sunlight
- Wearing a helmet with Fire Resistance effects (see the Datapack Basics chapter for details)
- Disabling the feature in the configuration

------

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why are my crops growing slowly?

Use a Growth Detector to check:

- Current season
- Humidity conditions
- Greenhouse level

------

## Why does Bone Meal still work?

This is intentional.

By default, Ecliptic Seasons does not completely disable Bone Meal. Seasons and humidity primarily affect natural crop growth rates.

------

## What do I need for out-of-season farming?

You will generally need:

- A greenhouse
- A Season Core
- Suitable humidity conditions

------

## What if snow cover conflicts with my resource pack?

Some resource packs and connected-texture mods modify block models, which may cause snow cover rendering issues.

You can:

- Disable snow cover rendering
- Use the Classic Snow Cover resource pack (1.20.1)
- Report a compatibility issue
